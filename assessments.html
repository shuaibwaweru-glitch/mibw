About Mental Health Screening

Why Assess?

Self-assessment helps you recognize symptoms, track changes over time, and decide if professional help is needed. Many conditions are highly treatable when caught early.

Confidential & Private

Your assessment results are completely private and confidential. They're stored securely and not shared with anyone without your consent.

Not a Diagnosis

These tools are screening instruments, not diagnostic. Only a licensed healthcare provider can diagnose a mental health condition.

Understanding Your Results

Assessment results typically fall into severity ranges. Here's what they generally mean:

Minimal Severity

Symptoms are present but may not significantly impact daily functioning. Continue with self-care and preventive practices.

Mild to Moderate Severity

Symptoms are affecting daily life. Consider speaking with a healthcare provider to explore treatment options.

Moderate to Severe

Symptoms are significantly impacting functioning. Professional treatment is strongly recommended. Consider reaching out to a therapist or physician.

Ready for Professional Support?

When to Seek Professional Help

You should consider reaching out to a mental health professional if:

  • Symptoms persist for more than 2 weeks
  • Symptoms interfere with work, school, or relationships
  • You're having thoughts of self-harm or suicide
  • You're using alcohol or drugs to cope
  • Symptoms are getting worse despite self-care efforts
  • You have a family history of mental health conditions
