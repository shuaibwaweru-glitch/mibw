Crisis Support Resources

988 Suicide & Crisis Lifeline

Call or Text: 988

24/7 support for anyone experiencing suicidal thoughts, emotional distress, or mental health crises. Free and confidential.

Crisis Text Line

Text: HOME to 741741

Text-based crisis support available 24/7. Trained counselors provide immediate help for those in emotional distress.

SAMHSA National Helpline

Call: 1-800-662-4357

Free, confidential support for substance use and mental health disorders. Available 24/7/365.

National Domestic Violence Hotline

Call: 1-800-799-7233

24/7 support for domestic violence situations. Confidential and multilingual assistance available.

Veterans Crisis Line

Call: 988 then press 1

Specialized support for veterans in crisis. Staffed by veterans and mental health professionals.

International Association for Suicide Prevention

Online Directory: iasp.info

International resources and crisis centers by country. Comprehensive global support network.

What to Do in a Crisis

If You're Having Suicidal Thoughts

  • Call 988 immediately (Suicide & Crisis Lifeline)
  • Text "HELLO" to 741741 (Crisis Text Line)
  • Call 911 if you're in immediate danger
  • Go to your nearest emergency room
  • Tell someone you trust - a friend, family member, or counselor
  • Remove means of harm if possible (medications, weapons, etc.)
  • Don't isolate - stay with someone or go to a public place

If Someone Else Is in Crisis

  • Listen without judgment - let them express what they're feeling
  • Take them seriously - don't minimize their pain
  • Call 988 together or help them get professional help
  • Stay with them - don't leave them alone
  • Remove access to means of harm if possible
  • Call 911 if there's immediate danger
  • Be patient and compassionate - recovery takes time

Warning Signs of Crisis

If you notice these signs in yourself or someone else, reach out for help immediately:

Emotional Signs

  • Severe hopelessness or despair
  • Intense fear or panic
  • Feeling trapped or helpless
  • Emotional numbness
  • Rage or anger

Behavioral Signs

  • Talking about death or suicide
  • Giving away possessions
  • Sudden personality changes
  • Risky or reckless behavior
  • Withdrawing from others

Physical Signs

  • Sleep changes (insomnia or oversleeping)
  • Loss of appetite
  • Increased substance use
  • Agitation or restlessness
  • Physical neglect

You Deserve Help

No matter what you're experiencing, support is available. You don't have to face this alone. Reach out to a crisis line, find a therapist, or talk to someone you trust today.
