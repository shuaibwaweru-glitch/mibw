Cost & Insurance

Insurance Accepted

Most therapists accept major insurance plans. Your out-of-pocket cost depends on your specific plan and deductible.

Tip: Call your insurance to verify coverage before scheduling.

Sliding Scale

Many therapists offer sliding scale fees based on income. Rates typically range from $30-80 per session.

Ask: About sliding scale options during your consultation call.

Private Pay

Self-pay sessions typically cost $100-250 per session, depending on therapist experience and location.

Note: Some offer package discounts for committing to multiple sessions.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I know if therapy is right for me?

If you're struggling with your mental health, relationships, work stress, or just want to understand yourself better, therapy can help. There's no "right" time to start - you're ready whenever you decide you want support.

What if I don't click with my first therapist?

That's normal! Therapy is a relationship, and finding the right fit matters. Most therapists understand if you decide it's not working and can help you find someone else.

Is therapy confidential?

Yes. Therapists are bound by confidentiality laws with very limited exceptions (imminent danger, abuse, court order). Your privacy is protected.

How often do I need to see a therapist?

Most people start with weekly sessions (50 minutes). As you progress, you might space them out. Your therapist will help you determine the right frequency for your needs.

Can I do therapy online?

Many of our therapists offer online video therapy, which is often just as effective as in-person. Check the "Format" on each profile to see their options.

What should I prepare for my first session?

Be honest about what brings you in. Most therapists will take time to understand your background, challenges, and goals. It's OK if you're nervous or unsure what to say.
